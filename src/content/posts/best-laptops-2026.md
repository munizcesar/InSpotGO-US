## Buying Guide

### Battery life vs performance
Apple Silicon (M4 Pro) is in a class of its own here — 16h+ with sustained performance. Windows laptops with discrete GPUs typically land at 6–10h under normal use. If you need all-day battery on Windows, choose the Flex 5i or Spectre x360.

### Display: OLED vs IPS vs XDR
OLED wins on contrast and black levels but can suffer burn-in with static UI elements over years. Apple's Liquid Retina XDR uses mini-LED with local dimming — no burn-in risk, very close to OLED quality. For budget buyers, IPS at 300+ nits is still perfectly usable.

### macOS vs Windows
If you're in the Adobe/Final Cut ecosystem, macOS's hardware-accelerated export is a real-world advantage. For gaming, Windows is the only viable choice. For everything else, personal preference.

### RAM: how much do you actually need?
- 8GB: Fine for basic tasks, browsing, office apps
- 16GB: Recommended minimum for developers, designers
- 32GB+: Video editing, running VMs, heavy multitasking
