## Why you need a password manager

The average person has 100+ online accounts. Reusing passwords is the #1 cause of account breaches — once one site leaks your password, attackers try it everywhere. A password manager generates and stores unique, strong passwords for every site so you only need to remember one master password.

## 1Password — Best overall

1Password is the most polished password manager available. It has excellent apps on every platform, a clean interface, and features that go beyond basic password storage.

### Key features
- **Watchtower** — scans your passwords for breaches, weak passwords and reused credentials
- **Travel Mode** — temporarily removes sensitive vaults when crossing borders
- **Secret Key** — two-factor protection built into the account setup (extra security layer)
- **1Password Families** — share vaults with up to 5 people, each with private and shared vaults
- **Browser extension** — works seamlessly across Chrome, Firefox, Safari and Edge
- **Passkey support** — store and use passkeys alongside traditional passwords

### 1Password pricing

1Password offers Individual, Families, Teams and Business plans. For current rates and promotions, visit the [1Password pricing page](https://1password.com/sign-up/).

### 1Password pros & cons
**Pros:** Best UI/UX, Travel Mode is unique, excellent family sharing, strong security model, great mobile apps.

**Cons:** No free plan, slightly more expensive than competitors.

## Dashlane — Best for extras

Dashlane bundles a password manager with a built-in VPN and dark web monitoring — making it a strong choice for users who want an all-in-one security suite.

### Key features
- **Built-in VPN** — powered by Hotspot Shield, included with Premium
- **Dark web monitoring** — scans the dark web for your email addresses and alerts you to breaches
- **Password health score** — gives your overall password hygiene a score and actionable fixes
- **Phishing alerts** — warns you when you're on a fake site
- **Password changer** — automatically updates passwords on supported sites

### Dashlane pricing

Dashlane offers Free, Premium, and Friends & Family plans. The free tier is limited to a small number of passwords and one device. For current rates, visit the [Dashlane pricing page](https://www.dashlane.com/plans).

### Dashlane pros & cons
**Pros:** VPN included, excellent dark web monitoring, clean interface, strong phishing protection.

**Cons:** Free plan is very limited, more expensive than 1Password and Bitwarden for individuals.

[🔐 Try Dashlane Free →](https://www.dashlane.com/plans)

## Bitwarden — Best free option

Bitwarden is the only fully open-source password manager on this list — its code is publicly audited, making it the most transparent option. The free plan is genuinely usable and better than most paid competitors.

### Key features
- **Fully open source** — audited by independent security researchers
- **Unlimited passwords on free plan** — across unlimited devices
- **End-to-end encryption** — zero-knowledge architecture
- **Self-hosting option** — run your own Bitwarden server for complete control
- **Send feature** — securely share passwords or files with anyone
- **Bitwarden Authenticator** — TOTP codes built-in (Premium)

### Bitwarden pricing

Bitwarden has a genuinely free tier (unlimited passwords, unlimited devices) and an affordable Premium plan that adds TOTP, file attachments and security reports. For current rates, visit the [Bitwarden pricing page](https://bitwarden.com/pricing/).

### Bitwarden pros & cons
**Pros:** Best free plan available, open source, self-hosting option, extremely affordable premium, strong security.

**Cons:** Interface less polished than 1Password, some features require Premium that competitors include for free.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Are password managers safe?
Yes — reputable password managers use zero-knowledge, end-to-end encryption. Even if the company is breached, attackers cannot access your passwords without your master password.

### What happens if I forget my master password?
Most password managers offer account recovery options. 1Password uses a Secret Key for recovery. Bitwarden allows you to set a hint. Always store your master password somewhere safe.

### Is Bitwarden really free?
Yes — Bitwarden's free plan includes unlimited passwords across unlimited devices, which is better than most paid tiers from competitors.

### Can I import passwords from my browser?
All three support importing from Chrome, Firefox, Safari and other browsers. The import process takes about 2 minutes.

### Should I use a password manager or just write passwords down?
A password manager is far safer. Written passwords can be lost, stolen or seen by others. Password managers encrypt everything and sync securely across all your devices.
