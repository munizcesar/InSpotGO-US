## Why use a VPN in 2026?

VPNs encrypt your internet traffic and mask your IP address. The most common use cases:

- **Privacy** — prevents ISPs, advertisers and hackers from tracking your activity
- **Public Wi-Fi security** — protects you on hotel, airport and café networks
- **Streaming** — access Netflix, BBC iPlayer and other services from other countries
- **Work remotely** — securely connect to company networks
- **Avoid geo-restrictions** — access content not available in your region

NordVPN is consistently ranked as the best all-around VPN. It has the largest server network, strong privacy credentials, and a feature set that covers everything from basic privacy to advanced threat protection.

## Surfshark — Best value

Surfshark offers the best value in the VPN market — unlimited simultaneous connections at a price well below competitors. For families or anyone with many devices, it's the obvious choice.

### Key features
- **Unlimited devices** — connect every device you own simultaneously
- **CleanWeb** — built-in ad and malware blocker
- **Alternative ID** — generates fake identity details to protect real info when signing up for services
- **Nexus** — routes traffic through a network of servers simultaneously (unique feature)
- **Audited no-logs policy**

### Surfshark pricing

Surfshark offers Starter, One and One+ plans. The 2-year plan includes several bonus months free. For current promotions and rates, visit the [Surfshark pricing page](https://surfshark.com/pricing).

### Surfshark pros & cons
**Pros:** Unlimited devices, cheapest reputable VPN, good speeds, strong privacy policy, excellent interface.

**Cons:** Smaller server network than NordVPN, speeds slightly inconsistent on some servers.

## ExpressVPN — Best for streaming

ExpressVPN consistently unblocks the most streaming services and has the most reliable performance for international content access. It's the most expensive on this list but justifies the price if streaming is your primary use case.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is using a VPN legal?
In most countries, yes. VPN use is legal in the US, UK, EU, Canada and Australia. Some countries restrict or ban VPNs — check local laws before use.

### Does a VPN slow down internet speed?
A small speed reduction is normal (5-15%). Premium VPNs like NordVPN and ExpressVPN have minimal impact on speeds over 100 Mbps connections.

### Can a VPN be hacked?
Reputable VPNs use AES-256 encryption which is effectively unbreakable. The bigger risk is choosing a VPN with a weak privacy policy — always pick providers with independently audited no-logs policies.

### Do free VPNs work?
Free VPNs are generally not recommended — they often log and sell your data, have slow speeds and limited servers. The VPNs on this list all offer 30-day money-back guarantees, making them risk-free to try.

### Which VPN is best for Netflix?
ExpressVPN and NordVPN are the most reliable for Netflix. Both consistently unblock Netflix US, UK, Japan and other regional libraries.
