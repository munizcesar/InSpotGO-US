## Testing Methodology

ANC performance measured with decibel reduction across frequencies (50Hz–8kHz) in three environments: airplane cabin (~80dB), busy coffee shop (~68dB), open-plan office (~58dB). Audio quality assessed via frequency response and A/B listening with reference tracks using LDAC and aptX Adaptive where supported.

## Sony WH-1000XM6 — Best Overall ANC

The XM6 remains the benchmark for ANC. Eight microphones feed an AI-powered adaptive algorithm that adjusts in real time to your environment — the result is the most consistent noise reduction across all frequency ranges tested.

**Specs:**
- Driver: 40mm dynamic
- Frequency response: 4Hz–40,000Hz
- ANC: AI adaptive, 8 microphones
- Codecs: LDAC, AAC, SBC
- Battery: 40h (ANC on) / 60h (ANC off)
- Weight: 254g
- Multipoint: 2 devices

**ANC performance (measured dB reduction):**
- Low (50–200Hz): 28dB
- Mid (200Hz–2kHz): 22dB
- High (2–8kHz): 18dB

**Strengths:** industry-leading ANC across all frequencies, 40h battery, excellent call quality, Speak-to-Chat auto-pause, 360 Reality Audio

**Weaknesses:** slightly plasticky feel for the price, touch controls occasionally misfire, no aptX

**Best for:** frequent flyers, commuters, open-plan office workers

## Bose QuietComfort Ultra — Most Comfortable

Bose has owned the comfort crown for years and the QC Ultra doesn't give it up. CustomTune ANC calibrates to your ear canal on every wear. Mid-frequency ANC (blocking voices) is the best tested — critical for office use.

**Specs:**
- Driver: 35mm TriPort acoustic
- ANC: CustomTune adaptive, 6 microphones
- Codecs: aptX Adaptive, AAC, SBC
- Battery: 24h (ANC on)
- Weight: 254g
- Clamping force: 2.8N (lowest tested)

**ANC performance:**
- Low (50–200Hz): 26dB
- Mid (200Hz–2kHz): 24dB ← best tested in this range
- High (2–8kHz): 16dB

**Strengths:** best comfort for extended wear, excellent voice-blocking ANC, premium build, intuitive controls, Immersive Audio mode

**Weaknesses:** 24h battery is the shortest here, less detail in sound vs Sony/Sennheiser, no LDAC

## Buying Guide

### ANC isn't equal across frequencies
All five headphones test well overall, but the specifics matter. Sony leads on low frequencies (engine hum, HVAC). Bose leads on mid frequencies (human voices, office chatter). If your main use case is an open-plan office, Bose wins. For flights, Sony wins.

### Codec matters more than specs suggest
If you stream at 320kbps or use a high-res source, LDAC (Sony) or aptX Adaptive (Bose, Sennheiser, B&W) make an audible difference over Bluetooth. iPhone users are capped at AAC regardless — AirPods Max is still the best choice there.

### Weight and long sessions
The AirPods Max at 384g cause noticeable fatigue after 2–3 hours for most users. If you wear headphones for 6+ hours, the Bose QC Ultra's clamping force and headband design are specifically engineered for that use case.
