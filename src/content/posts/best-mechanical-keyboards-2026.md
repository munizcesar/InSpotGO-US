## Switch Type Guide

### Linear Switches
**Characteristics:** Smooth keystroke, no tactile bump
**Sound:** Quiet to moderate
**Best For:** Gaming, fast typing, quiet environments
**Popular:** Cherry MX Red, Gateron Red

### Tactile Switches
**Characteristics:** Bump at actuation point
**Sound:** Moderate
**Best For:** Typing, programming, balanced use
**Popular:** Cherry MX Brown, Gateron Brown

### Clicky Switches
**Characteristics:** Tactile bump + audible click
**Sound:** Loud
**Best For:** Typing satisfaction, not shared spaces
**Popular:** Cherry MX Blue, Gateron Blue

## Buying Guide

### Layout Selection
**Full-size (104 keys):** Numpad users, data entry
**TKL (87 keys):** Balance of features and space
**75% (84 keys):** Compact with F-row and arrows
**60% (61 keys):** Maximum desk space, gaming focused

### Key Considerations
1. **Switch Type:** Try before buying if possible
2. **Hot-Swap:** Future-proof for switch changes
3. **Connectivity:** Wireless adds $20-30 premium
4. **Build Quality:** Metal > Plastic for durability
5. **Keycaps:** PBT > ABS for longevity
6. **Programmability:** QMK/VIA for power users

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Are mechanical keyboards worth it?**
A: Yes, if you type/game 2+ hours daily. Benefits: better typing feel, faster input, durability (50-100M keystrokes vs 5M membrane).

**Q: Which switches are quietest?**
A: Linear switches (Red) or specialized silent switches (Cherry MX Silent, Gateron Silent). Tactile Quiet also good option.

**Q: Can I use mechanical keyboard in office?**
A: Yes. Choose: Logitech MX Mechanical, linear switches, or add O-rings to dampen noise.

**Q: What is hot-swappable?**
A: Switches can be removed/replaced without soldering. Allows experimentation and easy repairs.

**Q: Do I need RGB lighting?**
A: Optional. Useful for low-light typing. Adds $10-20 to cost. White backlight sufficient for most.
