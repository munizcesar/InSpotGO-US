## Do you still need antivirus in 2026?

Windows Defender (built into Windows 10/11) is significantly better than it was 5 years ago and handles most basic threats. However, dedicated antivirus still adds meaningful protection for:

- **Ransomware protection** — dedicated behavioral analysis catches new variants faster
- **Phishing and web protection** — blocks malicious sites before they load
- **Identity theft monitoring** — alerts when your personal info appears in data breaches
- **VPN** — encrypted browsing on public networks
- **Performance** — some suites include cleanup and optimization tools

For power users who are careful with downloads, Windows Defender may be sufficient. For everyone else, the added protection is worth the cost.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Windows Defender good enough in 2026?
For basic users who are careful online, yes. For users who want ransomware remediation, VPN, dark web monitoring and identity protection, a paid suite adds meaningful value.

### Does antivirus slow down your computer?
Modern antivirus has minimal impact on performance. Bitdefender and Malwarebytes are particularly light. Norton has slightly more impact but is still acceptable on modern hardware.

### What's the best free antivirus?
Windows Defender is the best free antivirus for Windows users. Bitdefender Free (Windows only) and Malwarebytes Free (on-demand scanning) are also worth considering.

### Do Macs need antivirus?
Macs have strong built-in security but are not immune to malware. Bitdefender and Malwarebytes both offer excellent Mac protection at no extra cost on multi-device plans.

### Is Norton safe to use?
Yes — Norton is one of the most trusted security companies with a 30+ year track record. Its privacy policy is transparent and it undergoes regular third-party audits.
